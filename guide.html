Everything you need to know about using the We Do Tax Services Salary Calculator

Getting Started

The We Do Tax Services Salary Calculator is designed to help you calculate South African salaries accurately using official SARS tax tables. Follow these steps to get started:

Quick Start Tip

Visit the Test Page to see a pre-loaded example with real data. This is a great way to understand how the calculator works!

Step 1: Employee Information

Start by entering basic employee details:

Field Description Required
Employee Name Full name of the employee Optional (recommended for PDF)
Company Name Name of the employing company Optional (recommended for PDF)
Calculation Period Month and year (e.g., "January 2026") Optional (recommended for records)
Tax Year Select: 2024/2025, 2025/2026, or 2026/2027 Required

Step 2: Income Components

Enter all income received by the employee during the period:

Income Types Explained

Basic Salary
Monthly fixed salary before any additions
Overtime
Additional payments for work beyond normal hours
Annual Bonus
13th cheque or annual performance bonus
Other Bonuses
Commission, incentives, or other bonus payments
Subsistence Allowance
Daily allowance for meals and incidental costs
Travel Allowance
Monthly allowance for business travel
Other Allowances
Any other taxable allowances

Step 3: Fringe Benefits

Fringe benefits are taxable perks provided by the employer. These are added to your taxable income:

Important Note on Fringe Benefits

Fringe benefits increase your taxable income but are NOT deducted from your gross pay. They affect your PAYE calculation but don't reduce your take-home salary.

Fringe Benefit Types

Company Car Benefit
Value of company car for private use
Taxable Travel Portion
Portion of travel allowance subject to tax
Medical Aid Fringe Benefit
Employer medical aid contribution (taxable portion)
Provident Fund Fringe Benefit
Employer provident fund contribution (over threshold)
Other Fringe Benefits
Housing, loans, or other taxable benefits

Step 4: Employee Contributions

Enter amounts that are deducted from the employee's salary:

Deductible Contributions

Medical Aid Contribution
Employee's monthly medical aid contribution
Provident/Pension Fund
Employee's retirement fund contribution
Retirement Annuity
Private retirement annuity contribution
Medical Aid Dependents
Number of dependents (for tax credit calculation)

Medical Aid Tax Credit

The calculator automatically applies medical aid tax credits: R364 for main member, R364 for first dependent, and R246 for each additional dependent.

Step 5: Employer Contributions

These are additional costs borne by the employer (not deducted from employee salary):

Employer Costs

Medical Aid - Employer
Employer's contribution to medical aid
Provident Fund - Employer
Employer's contribution to provident fund
UIF - Employer (Auto)
Automatically calculated at 1% (capped at R177.12)
Skills Development Levy (Auto)
Automatically calculated at 1% of gross income

Understanding Your Results

After calculation, you'll see comprehensive results divided into sections:

Gross Pay
Example

Total Deductions
Example

Net Pay
Example

Detailed Breakdowns Include:

  • Income Breakdown: All income components with totals
  • Fringe Benefits: All taxable benefits
  • Tax Calculation: Taxable income, tax brackets, rebates, and effective rate
  • Deductions: PAYE, UIF, and all employee contributions
  • Employer Contributions: All employer costs including UIF and SDL

Generating PDF Reports

Once calculations are complete, you can download a professional PDF report:

PDF Report Features

Reports include We Do Tax Services branding, all calculation details, employee information, tax breakdowns, and a professional layout perfect for sharing with clients or keeping records.

To download a PDF:

  1. Complete the calculation
  2. Review the results on screen
  3. Click the "Download PDF Report" button
  4. The PDF will be saved to your device automatically

Tips & Best Practices

Save Your Work

Download PDF reports for record-keeping. The calculator doesn't store data between sessions.

Double-Check Entries

Review all inputs before calculating. Small errors in input can lead to significant differences in results.

Select Correct Tax Year

Always ensure you've selected the appropriate tax year for accurate SARS compliance.
